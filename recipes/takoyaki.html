Takoyaki

Takoyaki Recipe Description

Takoyaki is one of the most popular street snacks in Japan, aside from yakisoba noodles and onigiri rice balls.
Little roadside stalls can be found everywhere, ready to serve up a batch of takoyaki when you need it most.
But for the freshest, tastiest takoyaki, the best thing to do is to make it yourself.

Here is a list of ingredients:

  • Batter
  • Octopus
  • Spring Onion
  • Red Pickled Ginger
  • Tenkasu (crispy fried tempura batter)

Steps to make Takoyaki

  1. Slice the octopus into small p bite-size pieces
  2. In a bowl combine the plain and corn flour in a bowl, then in a separate bowl whisk the egg
  3. Add the egg to the dry mixture, then pour in the dashi stock or water and whisk
  4. Now, heat the takoyaki pan on medium-high heat and pass oil gently over the entire surface
  5. Pour the batter into the center of the pan and pour until all the holes are full the batter covers the entire plate
  6. Place a piece of octopus or chicken into each hole, then sporadically spread the spring onion, ginger and tempura crumbs
    across the pan
  7. Move your chopstick in an ‘L’ shape around each hole, then immediately push down into the well to flip the batter,
    forming a rough ball shape as you go.
  8. Keep on flipping each ball until the batter is crisp and shows a golden brown on all sides
